Druva

Designing handoff, licensing, and role-specific workflows between Druva, MSP partners, and end customers while improving privacy, provisioning clarity, and operational scale.

Project Context

What is Druva?

Druva is a cloud-native backup and data security platform that helps businesses protect, manage, and recover data across endpoints, SaaS apps, data centers, and cloud workloads.

What is MSP?

MSPs are Druva’s service partners. They are partner companies that use Druva to manage their customers.

What is this project about?

The project focused on creating a smooth handoff between Druva and MSPs. Druva handled the initial setup, and MSPs took over customer management after that.

Persona 1 : MSC Partners

Who They are

A partner-side operations admin who manages multiple customer accounts through Druva’s partner dashboard.

What they do

  • Set up new customers
  • Assign products and licenses
  • Manage backup and restore operations
  • Monitor reports and usage
  • Control customer access and operational setup

Goals

  • Manage multiple customers from one place
  • Complete setup quickly
  • Reduce dependency on Druva support
  • Clearly understand product and access levels available

Pain Points

  • Additional products are harder to provision
  • Provisioning rules differ across customer types
  • Access states can be confusing
  • Invalid conversions can disrupt operations

Needs

I want more product modules in MSC so I can offer more services and attract more customers.

Persona 2: Customers

Who They are

The end customer admin whose environment is managed by the MSP.

What they do

  • Oversee their organization’s setup
  • Ensure the right products are enabled
  • Review account access and visibility
  • Protect sensitive backup and reporting information

Goals

  • Get required products enabled quickly
  • Maintain privacy and control over the console
  • Avoid giving unnecessary visibility to the MSP
  • Ensure setup works without confusion

Pain Points

  • May not want MSPs to view reports or backup details
  • Product availability can depend on backend processes
  • Differences between provisioning models are not always visible

Needs

I want my data and reports to remain private, without full MSP visibility into my console.

Persona 3 : Druva Support

Who They are

An internal Druva team member who supports provisioning, licensing dependencies, and advanced product enablement.

What they do

  • Provision customers when licensing requires Druva involvement
  • Support additional products and workloads
  • Resolve account mapping and access issues
  • Handle cases the MSP cannot complete directly

Goals

  • Enable customers quickly and accurately
  • Reduce manual operational effort
  • Support product expansion without creating process bottlenecks
  • Make provisioning dependencies easier to manage

Pain Points

  • Adding new products requires extra licensing effort
  • Manual intervention does not scale well
  • Unclear handoffs between Druva and MSPs create delays
  • Unsupported transitions can cause operational issues

Needs

A scalable model where Druva-led provisioning and MSP-led management work together smoothly.

Problem Breakdown: User Needs Card

User Needs

MSP

I want more product modules in MSC so I can offer more services and attract more customers.

Customer

I want my data and reports to remain private, with full control of my console visibility.

Druva

Adding more products requires new license types and operational effort, which is difficult to scale quickly.

Problem Statement

  • MSC supported only limited products, restricting MSP offerings and risking customer loss.
  • Customers wanted privacy and did not want MSPs to have full access to their consoles and reports.

Technical Difficulties

  • Introducing multi-level licensing came with operational and technical complexity, making it difficult to scale quickly within MSC.
  • As a temporary solution, Druva provisioned these products directly on behalf of MSC.
  • The design needed to support an immediate workaround while preparing a future state where the entire flow lives inside MSC.
Use Cases
Restricted Access Unrestricted Access
MSC-Provisioned Users can provision licenses for supported core products, but access remains limited to the managed-service model. Additional products and advanced workloads are not available under this setup. MSP admins can provision licenses, access the console, manage customer accounts, perform operational tasks, and view reports for supported core offerings.
Druva-Provisioned Users can provision licenses and complete provisioning-related steps, but they cannot access the console afterward to manage the account or view reports. This is because provisioning ownership and certain operational controls remain on Druva's side, and the experience depends on Druva-side licensing and technical enablement. Users can provision licenses, access the console, manage the customer account, and view reports once the customer has been fully enabled under the Druva-Provisioned model. This model also supports access to additional workloads and features not available for MSC-Provisioned customers.

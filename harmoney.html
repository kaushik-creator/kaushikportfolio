Story
From Listing Site to Trading Platform

Harmoney was positioned as a bond listing website, not a digital bond trading engine. The challenge was to evolve it into a product traders could use in their everyday workflow. Since bond traders often depend on four to six screens for different tasks, Harmoney aimed to reduce this fragmentation and earn a place as one essential screen in their trading environment.

Understanding the Product

At the beginning, Harmoney primarily worked as an informational bond-search website while actual trading happened offline through calls and manual follow-ups.

The goal was to transform Harmoney into a complete digital trading platform where users could discover bonds, compare options, place orders, and track execution in one connected workflow.

This storyboard became the alignment tool for design and engineering, helping us prioritize the most critical user flows first.

The Problem
Bond Trading Was Broken

Brokers were still making deals over phone calls. Seriously. They'd call each other, negotiate, hang up, and manually record the deal in a spreadsheet. If prices changed while they were on the call with someone else, they had no way to know. Wealth managers had zero visibility into what bonds were available. It was chaos.

The CEO (who came from this industry) knew we could digitize this. But designing for institutional traders is hard — they care about speed and data density, not pretty interfaces.

What I Learned
The Hard Way

Working at an early-stage startup as the only designer was brutal. No design manager, no design ops, no safety net. But it taught me things a bigger company never would.

01
You have to learn the domain. I spent weeks learning how bond trading actually works. Not just skimming Wikipedia. Actually understanding what the CEO was building. That's when design decisions stopped being guesses.
02
Systems matter more than screens. I couldn't design every state of every component. So I built a system that let the engineers extrapolate. That was more valuable than a perfect Figma file.
03
Shipping beats perfection. We launched with "good enough" design. Then I fixed things based on how real traders actually used it. It was messy, but it worked better than if I'd obsessed over perfection for 6 months.
